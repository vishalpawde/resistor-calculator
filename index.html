What is a Resistor?

A resistor is a fundamental electronic component that limits or controls electric current in a circuit. The coloured bands printed on its body encode its resistance value, multiplier, and tolerance — a universal language engineers use worldwide.

⚡ Electric current flow
💡

Did You Know? The colour band system was standardised by the IEC (International Electrotechnical Commission) in the 1920s.

🎓

Mnemonic: "Bad Beer Rots Our Young Guts But Vodka Goes Well" — Black, Brown, Red, Orange, Yellow, Green, Blue, Violet, Grey, White.

📐

Unit: Resistance is measured in Ohms (Ω), named after German physicist Georg Simon Ohm.

The Formula

Where D1 = First digit, D2 = Second digit
